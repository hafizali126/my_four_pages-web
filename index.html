  1. The Tortoise and the Hare (Aesop):

    A fast but arrogant hare mocks a slow tortoise. They race, and the hare, confident in his speed, takes a nap. The persistent tortoise continues walking and wins the race, demonstrating that slow and steady wins the race.



  2. The Boy Who Cried Wolf:

    A bored shepherd boy lies to villagers about a wolf attacking his sheep. When a real wolf appears, no one believes his pleas for help. The story teaches that liars are not believed even when they speak the truth.
